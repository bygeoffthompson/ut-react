Perfect Your eCommerce Growth Strategy with These 20 Must-Have Features

By morgan on Oct 3, 2024

An effective eCommerce growth strategy involves more than just having a website to sell products—it’s about creating an engaging, user-friendly experience that drives conversions and enhances customer satisfaction. Implementing the right eCommerce widgets can help you achieve this. These tools improve everything from navigation to customer support, increasing the efficiency and appeal of your site.

Flaunt a new eCommerce growth strategy and check out the top features and widgets to consider for when developing your eCommerce website!


1. Search Bar with Autocomplete

A search bar is a basic yet crucial widget that helps users find products quickly. When enhanced with an autocomplete feature, it suggests relevant products as customers type, leading to faster results and a smoother shopping experience.

  • Pros: Improves product discovery and enhances the user journey, aligning with a strong eCommerce growth strategy.
  • Cons: Can sometimes slow down page speed if not optimized properly.

2. Product Reviews and Ratings

Customer reviews and ratings provide social proof that helps other users make informed purchasing decisions. Displaying authentic feedback from past buyers builds trust and can significantly increase conversions.

  • Pros: Builds credibility and trust, increases conversions.
  • Cons: Negative reviews, if not managed properly, can deter potential customers.

3. Live Chat Support

A live chat widget allows customers to get real-time assistance with their queries. Many live chat tools now integrate AI-powered chatbots that provide 24/7 support for common questions.

  • Pros: Improves customer service, reduces cart abandonment, and provides instant assistance.
  • Cons: Poorly configured chatbots can frustrate customers if they don’t resolve issues effectively.


4. Wishlist

A wishlist widget enables users to save items they’re interested in for future purchases. It’s especially useful for customers who aren’t ready to buy but want to keep track of products.

  • Pros: Encourages return visits and future purchases.
  • Cons: Items left in wishlists may never convert to sales.

5. Product Filtering

Filters help users sort products based on criteria like price, color, size, and brand. This widget is essential for sites with large product catalogs, making it easier for customers to find what they need.

  • Pros: Improves user experience and helps customers find products faster.
  • Cons: Complex filters can confuse users if not designed intuitively.

6. Social Media Integration

Widgets that integrate social media sharing buttons allow customers to share products directly to platforms like Facebook, Instagram, or Pinterest. These widgets not only increase brand visibility but also drive social proof.

  • Pros: Expands brand reach and engages new potential customers.
  • Cons: If too prominent, they can clutter the user interface and distract from the purchase process.

7. Currency Converter

A currency converter widget automatically adjusts prices based on the user’s location or preferred currency, providing a seamless shopping experience for international customers.

  • Pros: Encourages global sales by making pricing transparent and understandable.
  • Cons: Incorrect exchange rates or poor integration can lead to pricing discrepancies.

8. Product Recommendation Engine

Product recommendation widgets use AI algorithms to suggest related or complementary products to customers. Based on browsing history, cart items, or popular products, these widgets help increase average order value.

  • Pros: Boosts upselling and cross-selling opportunities.
  • Cons: Incorrect recommendations may frustrate users or appear irrelevant.


9. Pop-Up for Email Capture

Pop-ups that encourage visitors to sign up for newsletters or promotions help build your email marketing list. Offering an incentive, such as a discount or exclusive offer, can increase sign-up rates.

  • Pros: Grows your email list, boosts customer retention through targeted marketing.
  • Cons: Can annoy users and lead to higher bounce rates if too intrusive.

10. Product Comparison Tool

A comparison widget allows users to compare multiple products side-by-side, making it easier for them to evaluate options before making a purchase decision.

  • Pros: Reduces decision fatigue and helps customers make informed choices.
  • Cons: Can slow down website performance if not optimized properly.

11. Abandoned Cart Reminder

An abandoned cart widget sends email reminders to users who added products to their cart but didn’t complete the purchase. Some tools also offer push notifications or text messages to re-engage users.

  • Pros: Recovers lost sales and increases conversion rates.
  • Cons: Repeated reminders may annoy potential customers.


12. Loyalty Program Widget

Loyalty program widgets allow users to sign up for and manage their rewards points or perks, encouraging repeat business. They typically offer points for purchases, reviews, or social sharing.

  • Pros: Increases customer retention and incentivizes repeat purchases.
  • Cons: Can be complicated to set up and track effectively.

13. Trust Badges and Security Seals

Trust badges, such as SSL certificates and secure payment logos, give customers confidence that their data is safe. Displaying these badges prominently reassures customers during the checkout process.

  • Pros: Builds trust and reduces cart abandonment.
  • Cons: If overused or placed poorly, they can clutter the checkout process.

14. Shipping Calculator

A shipping calculator widget allows customers to estimate shipping costs based on their location and the items in their cart. This is especially useful for international customers who may face higher shipping fees.

  • Pros: Reduces cart abandonment by providing transparency about shipping costs.
  • Cons: If not updated frequently, shipping estimates can become inaccurate.

15. Exit-Intent Pop-Up

An exit-intent pop-up detects when a user is about to leave your website and displays a targeted message, such as an exclusive offer or discount, to encourage them to stay or make a purchase.

  • Pros: Reduces bounce rates and increases conversions by giving visitors a reason to stay.
  • Cons: May be seen as intrusive if not presented in a user-friendly way.

16. Mobile-Friendly Navigation Menu

Mobile-friendly navigation is essential for creating a seamless user experience on smaller screens. A mobile-friendly navigation menu ensures that your website is easy to use on all devices, supporting a broader eCommerce growth strategy that includes mobile optimization.

  • Pros: Enhances user experience for mobile shoppers and improves engagement.
  • Cons: If not properly designed, mobile navigation can be hard to use.

17. Order Tracking Widget

This widget allows customers to track their orders directly from your website, providing real-time updates on shipping and delivery statuses.

  • Pros: Enhances post-purchase experience and reduces customer service inquiries.
  • Cons: Incorrect tracking information can lead to frustration and poor customer experience.

18. Payment Method Icons

Displaying icons for available payment methods (e.g., Visa, PayPal, Apple Pay) at checkout reassures customers that their preferred payment option is accepted.

  • Pros: Increases trust and encourages checkout completion.
  • Cons: Overuse of icons can clutter the checkout page.


19. Back-in-Stock Notification

When an item is out of stock, this widget allows users to sign up for alerts when it becomes available again. A back-in-stock notification widget keeps customers engaged by alerting them when out-of-stock items become available—an excellent tool to support a robust eCommerce growth strategy.

  • Pros: Encourages future sales and maintains customer interest.
  • Cons: Delayed notifications can result in missed sales opportunities.

20. Gift Card Widget

Offering digital gift cards through a dedicated widget allows customers to purchase and send gift cards directly from your website. This is a popular option during holiday seasons and for special occasions.

  • Pros: Increases sales and provides customers with a flexible gift-giving option.
  • Cons: Tracking gift card usage can be difficult without proper integration.

Perfecting Your eCommerce Growth Strategy with Must-Have Features

Incorporating these essential widgets into your eCommerce website can dramatically improve user experience, drive higher conversions, and support your overall eCommerce growth strategy. From search bars and product recommendations to shipping calculators and live chat, these tools are designed to make shopping more convenient and engaging for your customers.

Leveraging the right mix of features will enhance both functionality and customer satisfaction, elevating your eCommerce business and ensuring long-term growth. By integrating these widgets into your eCommerce website design, you’ll create a streamlined, user-friendly platform that’s optimized for success.
